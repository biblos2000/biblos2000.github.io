Integral Library Management

Biblos 2000 Profesional

Biblos 2000 Professional is a software developed for the comprehensive automated management of libraries. Ideal for both personal (home) libraries and specialized and institutional libraries; to keep a reliable record of the cultural heritage available, as well as the technical and circulation processes that can be carried out around the documents. Now with the possibility to tackle work in networks, using MySQL databases to provide greater quality and consistency in information retrieval services.



What can we do with Biblos 2000 Pro?

Cataloging
It allows the unambiguous and unambiguous description of the documents, providing the necessary elements for their identification and adjusting to the regulations that regulate the process (RCAA 2, ISBD).


Classification
To describe the main topic that a document deals with based on the standardized classification language that the documentalist selects, they have the tool by which the main topic is determined and the corresponding index (notation) is assigned for that topic.
In the Configuration module you will find that you can classify materials using two options:

* DDC (Dewey Decimal Classification, sumaries)
* UDC (Universal Decimal Classification, Summary)

In order to carry out a methodical INDEXING, the program puts at your disposal the alphabetical edition of the UNESCO THESAURUS.


Queries (OPAC)
It makes it possible to retrieve information through a simple, intuitive and efficient search.


Circulation
Registers and organizes the loans made and the current users of the information unit.


Setting
It offers customization tools for Biblos 2000 Pro and its main functions. It includes backups, selection of the classification system, export of records and maintenance of databases, inventory, statistics, etc.



Who is it designed for?


It has been created to be implemented as a basic tool for the automation of library technical processes by:

  • LIBRARIANS or related technicians,
  • ADVANCED STUDENTS of Library Science,
  • COLLECTORS of books,
  • PEOPLE in general interested in automatically and efficiently managing their collections.



Who is responsible for its creation and development?

It was designed in an event-oriented programming environment, using the NeoBook program. The official creator is Ricardo Martín Contard, an Argentine documentary library technician; currently, professor in Classification and Indexing, at the Autonomous University of Entre Ríos (UADER).
